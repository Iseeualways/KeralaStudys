All About GATE Exam

The Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) is one of the primary national-level examinations conducted in India for admission to postgraduate courses across streams such as Engineering, Technology, and Architecture. It is also conducted for admissions to postgraduate and doctorate courses in commerce, design, arts, humanities, and science offered by various IITs, IIMs, and Centrally Funded Technical Institutions (CFTIs).

In addition to academic admissions, GATE scores are widely used by Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs) such as ONGC, NTPC, IOCL, and BHEL as part of their recruitment process. The examination is jointly organised by the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) and seven Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) on a rotational basis.

The GATE exam is open to candidates who have completed or are currently in the final year of their bachelorâ€™s degree in engineering, technology, architecture, or relevant science disciplines. It evaluates a candidateâ€™s understanding of core subjects studied during their undergraduate programme, along with their analytical and problem-solving abilities.

Qualifying the GATE exam can also create opportunities for higher studies abroad, financial assistance, research fellowships, and enhanced career prospects, making it a valuable examination for engineering and science graduates.
